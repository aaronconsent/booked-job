# COURSE 8 — PAID ADS WITHOUT LIGHTING MONEY ON FIRE
### Booked / booked-jobs.com · Marketing 101 · Course 8
**Voice:** same dead-level foreman. Swearing light, on the punchlines.

---

## THE NARRATION (TTS-ready)

**BEAT 1 — COLD OPEN**

VO: Of every door in this course, paid ads is where contractors light money on fire the fastest. Thousands of dollars, gone, with nothing to show but a pretty report. But done right, that first ad dollar can come back with friends. So let's spend it like adults — without torching it.

`ON SCREEN: "PAID ADS WITHOUT BURNING CASH" — Course 8`

---

**BEAT 2 — SEARCH ADS = CATCHING INTENT**

VO: We're talking search ads — the ones at the top of Google. And here's why they beat a billboard. A billboard hopes somebody needs you someday. A search ad puts you in front of a guy who is typing emergency plumber near me, right now, with water on the floor. You're not interrupting him. You're answering the door he just knocked on. That's the highest intent there is.

`ON SCREEN: search ads = caught at "near me", right now`

---

**BEAT 3 — WHERE THE MONEY BLEEDS**

VO: Now, a click in the home trades runs you about six, seven bucks, sometimes a lot more. And the money bleeds out three ways. One, broad keywords — you pay six bucks for some kid Googling how to fix a toilet himself. Two, no tracking, so you have no clue what worked. And three, slow callbacks, which we'll get to, because that one's a killer.

`ON SCREEN: bleeds → broad keywords · no tracking · slow callbacks`

---

**BEAT 4 — THE MISSED-CALL TRAP**

VO: Here's the one that makes me crazy. You pay six, seven dollars for the click. The ad works. The phone rings. And nobody picks up. You just paid for a lead and threw it in the trash. A missed call on a paid ad isn't a missed call — it's a two-hundred-dollar job you set on fire. The ad did its job. You didn't.

`ON SCREEN: paid click + missed call = $200 on fire`

---

**BEAT 5 — START TINY**

VO: So how do you not get burned? You start tiny. Do not dump five grand in on day one. Set a small daily budget you won't cry over. Use tight keywords — emergency plumber, not just plumbing. Pick one city. And then you actually watch it, like it's a space heater near the curtains. Small, tight, and watched.

`ON SCREEN: small budget · tight keywords · one city · WATCH it`

---

**BEAT 6 — TRACK & KILL**

VO: Then you track everything, and you judge it by the same number as always — cost per booked job. Not clicks, not calls. Booked jobs. Some keywords will print money. Most will just spend it. Find the few winners, pour the budget into those, and kill the losers without mercy. That's the whole game — feed what works, starve what doesn't.

`ON SCREEN: track by CPBJ → feed winners, KILL losers`

---

**BEAT 7 — DO THIS NOW**

VO: And here's the homework, and it comes before you spend a single dollar. Make sure every call gets answered. By you, by a real person, or by a service that picks up. Because running ads to a phone nobody answers is the dumbest money in this entire course. Fix the phone first. Then, and only then, turn on the ads.

`ON SCREEN: ANSWER THE PHONE before you spend a dime`

---

**BEAT 8 — CLOSE / BRIDGE TO COURSE 9**

VO: That's paid ads. Gas you control — start small, track by booked jobs, kill the losers, and for God's sake answer the phone. Do that and ads stop being a fire and start being a faucet. Next, we get you found not just on Google, but by the A I tools people are starting to ask instead. That's Course nine. See you there.

`ON SCREEN: NEXT → Course 9: Get Found by Google AND AI`
